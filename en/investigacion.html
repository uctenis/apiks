Research Lines

Our Research

Analyzing the multifaceted nature of the academic profession in Chile from an international comparative perspective.

Academic Career Gender & Academia Teaching Scientific Research Leadership Internationalization

Academic Career

Line 1

This line explores contractual conditions, employment security, and promotional pathways within Chilean universities. It focuses on understanding how academics perceive their prospects for development and job security, and how these variables associate with their overall scientific productivity and professional well-being.

Core Research Questions:

  • What are the differences in tenure and job security between state-owned, private-traditional, and private-non-traditional Chilean universities?
  • How does contractual precariousness affect the mental health and job satisfaction of university faculty in Chile?
  • How do higher education institutions structure promotions and career progression incentives?

Gender & Academia

Line 2

We investigate gender gaps within the Chilean higher education system. Our focus lies on differences in research output, vertical segregation in academic hierarchies (the glass ceiling effect), and specific barriers encountered by female academics during formative and early-career stages.

Core Research Questions:

  • How are teaching vs. research hours distributed between male and female faculty members in national universities?
  • What structural mechanisms and invisible barriers explain the underrepresentation of women in Full Professor ranks?
  • What are the main work-life balance challenges reported by female academics during doctoral training and early-career placement?

University Teaching

Line 3

We analyze the educational role of faculty, addressing pedagogical practices, active learning methodologies, and self-perceptions of educational quality and social impact. We also study how administrative burdens and accreditation pressures shape daily classroom practices.

Core Research Questions:

  • What proportion of actual working hours do Chilean academics allocate to teaching compared to their contract designations?
  • How does institutional pedagogical training correlate with innovation in university classrooms?
  • What factors determine the institutional value and prestige academics associate with teaching relative to scientific research?

Scientific Research

Line 4

This line concentrates on research capacity, scientific output metrics, access to public competitive research funding (e.g., Fondecyt, Fondef), and the transfer and dissemination of knowledge to societal and business sectors.

Core Research Questions:

  • How do team size and institutional infrastructure affect high-impact scientific output?
  • What financial and non-financial incentives are most effective in boosting publications in main indexed databases (WoS/Scopus)?
  • How are technology transfer and public science communication structured within Chilean universities?

University Leadership

Line 5

This area evaluates university governance, administrative leadership styles, the degree of faculty participation in institutional decision-making, and the impact of managerialism and bureaucratization on academic autonomy.

Core Research Questions:

  • How does the Chilean faculty perceive the impact of New Public Management (NPM) on their research and teaching autonomy?
  • To what extent do democratic representation mechanisms influence job satisfaction in public institutions?
  • What is the role of ministerial directives and national accreditation boards in local university decisions?

Internationalization

Line 6

We study the integration of Chilean academics into the global scientific community. This covers physical and virtual mobility, co-authorship with international peers, global research network membership, and joint doctoral programs, while analyzing geographic and financial barriers specific to the Global South.

Core Research Questions:

  • What factors most effectively foster internationalization among early-career academics compared to senior faculty?
  • How do joint international doctoral agreements contribute to strengthening local research capabilities?
  • How do funding constraints and geographical distance in the Global South limit international academic mobility?
