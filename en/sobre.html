About APIKS

The world's largest international research consortium on the academic profession.

Introduction

Researching higher education in the knowledge society

The Academic Profession in the Knowledge-Based Society (APIKS) is a large-scale comparative scientific study that examines structural changes, challenges, and labor dynamics experienced by academics across different regions of the globe.

It was created to generate a deep diagnosis of how the transition toward knowledge-based economies redefines the social role of faculty, their responsibilities, priorities, and the institutional environments where they practice.

Global Network

An alliance of more than 30 nations

Through a robust network of national consortia, APIKS groups researchers from leading universities in Europe, America, Asia, Africa, and Oceania. This diversity allows contrasting highly disparate geopolitical and institutional realities, from long-established higher education systems to those in developmental stages.

The consortium meets regularly twice a year at international conferences to share analysis, coordinate data collection, and plan high-impact joint publications. In January 2023, Chile had the prestige of hosting the meeting for the first time.

Methodology

The scientific value of the APIKS dataset

The study's primary scientific tool is a standardized survey applied to representative national samples. The unified dataset generated in 2018-2019 consolidated information from 20 key countries: Argentina, Canada, Chile, Croatia, Estonia, Finland, Germany, Kazakhstan, Japan, South Korea, Lithuania, Malaysia, Mexico, Portugal, Russia, Slovenia, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, and Turkey.

This survey explores essential dimensions such as:

  • Career path, academic ranks, and employment stability.
  • Distribution of time between teaching, research, and institutional service.
  • Internationalization practices and global scientific collaboration networks.
  • Influence of institutional governance and funding pressures.
  • Gender gaps, equity, and barriers in early career stages.
